Resources & Datasets

Look through the datasets below (and feel free to add your own ideas). Pick one for practice today; your final choice can change later.

  • IPUMS https://ipums.org
    • Microdata on people & households across time and countries. Rich demographics, labor, education, housing.
  • World Bank Data https://data.worldbank.org
    • Hundreds of economic & development indicators (GDP, inflation, enrollment) by country and year.
  • FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) https://fred.stlouisfed.org
    • US macro time series (CPI, unemployment, rates) with strong metadata and consistent updates.
  • Open Sports Data https://github.com/statsbomb/open-data
    • Event- and season-level data for soccer & baseball. Great for cross-sectional comparisons and time series performance.
  • IPEDS (US Higher Ed) https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/use-the-data
    • Institution-level data: enrollment, completion, costs, faculty, finances. Useful for campus-level questions.
  • BLS (Labor Statistics) https://www.bls.gov/data/
    • Employment, wages, CPI, productivity. National, state, metro breakdowns.
  • CRSP Center for Research in Security Prices
    • US equity prices, returns, and events. Ideal for time-series & panel analyses at the firm or market level.
  • Compustat
    • Firm-level fundamentals (income statement, balance sheet). Great for corporate finance & accounting questions.
  • Revelio Labs
    • Human Resources/Workforce database (professional profiles/ job postings/ employee sentiment reviews etc).
  • Card, D., & Krueger, A. B. (1994). Minimum Wages and Employment: A Case Study of the Fast-Food Industry in New Jersey and Pennsylvania https://davidcard.berkeley.edu/papers/njmin-aer.pdf
    • American Economic Review, 84 (4), 772793.
  • Angrist, J. D., & Krueger, A. B. (1991). Does Compulsory School Attendance Affect Schooling and Earnings? https://academic.oup.com/qje/article-abstract/106/4/979/1873496
    • Quarterly Journal of Economics, 106 (4), 9791014.
  • Bertrand, M., & Mullainathan, S. (2004). Are Emily and Greg More Employable Than Lakisha and Jamal? A Field Experiment on Labor Market Discrimination https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257%2F0002828042002561
    • American Economic Review, 94 (4), 9911013.
  • Duflo, E. (2001). Schooling and Labor Market Consequences of School Construction in Indonesia: Evidence from an Unusual Policy Experiment https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257%2Faer.91.4.795
    • American Economic Review, 91 (4), 795813.
  • Chetty, R., Friedman, J. N., & Rockoff, J. E. (2014). Measuring the Impacts of Teachers II: Teacher Value-Added and Student Outcomes in Adulthood https://pubs.aeaweb.org/doi/pdfplus/10.1257/aer.104.9.2633
    • Quarterly Journal of Economics, 129 (1), 263320.
  • Jensen, R. (2010). The (Perceived) Returns to Education and the Demand for Schooling https://academic.oup.com/qje/article-abstract/125/2/515/1882172
    • Quarterly Journal of Economics, 125 (2), 515548.

Ethics: Prefer official downloads/APIs; anonymize sensitive info; document cleaning decisions.
